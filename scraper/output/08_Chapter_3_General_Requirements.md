# Chapter 3 General Requirements

About this chapter:Chapter 3 provides general requirements for asphalt kettles, combustible waste material, ignition sources, motion picture projection rooms and film,open burning,recreational fires,portable outdoor fireplaces, open flames,powered industrial trucksand equipment, smoking, vacant premises, vehicle impact protection, fueled equipment,indoordisplays, miscellaneous storage, outdoor pallet storage, hazards to fire fighters, roof gardens and landscaped roofs, laundry carts andmobile food preparation vehicles. These are intended to improve premises safety for everyone, including construction workers, tenants, operations and maintenance personnel, and emergency response personnel.

Hot ashes, cinders, smoldering coals or greasy or oily materials subject to spontaneous ignition shall not be deposited in a combustible receptacle, within 10 feet (3048 mm) of other combustible material including combustible walls and partitions or within 2 feet (610 mm) of openings to buildings.

Exception:The minimum required separation distance to other combustible materials shall be 2 feet (610 mm) where the material is deposited in a covered, noncombustible receptacle placed on a noncombustible floor, ground surface or stand.

- Constructed ofsteelnot less than 4 inches (102 mm) in diameter and concrete filled.
